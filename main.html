OneFourFive is a Virtual Private Network (VPN) based anonymizer that goes to incredible lengths to keep you secure, anonymous, and invisible on the Internet. The basic idea is simple: Take all of your traffic to the Internet, encrypt it at a very high level, and transport it to a far-away location before sending it out to its destination. This means that all of the data leaving your home or office is scrambled, and can't be examined or controlled in any way. Furthermore, the data is only unscrambled after it leaves OneFourFive's servers, and is mixed with traffic from all of the rest of our users from all over the world.

OneFourFive also includes an optional Tor gateway service with the anonymizer, which then re-encrypts the traffic as it leaves and sends it to a randomized Tor node to be decrypted and forwarded to its destination. This means that even if the provider used by OneFourFive is compromised, your data is still secure. Since the data is encrypted and tunnelled through the provider, even our provider (or the government of our provider) cannot examine your data.

Incredible Lengths?

All of the things mentioned so far may sound exotic, but are just the beginning. OneFourFive takes anonymity to another level due to a holistic approach. Everything, from the country of Incorporation to the distribution of profits is designed to increase the anonymity of the whole system:

Servers hosted in Iceland
Well known for their strong stance on privacy and anti-censorship, Iceland is a champion for the values OneFourFive treasures.
No personal information collected - EVER
To create an account, you need no information. We will generate a random, memorable username for you and take your payment and password. Want to cut your ties and start fresh? Do that at any time. Create one account or one thousand. Under no circumstances to we ever need - nor want - any of your personal information.
Preferred payment mechanism is Bitcoin
As the premier secure, completely anonymous payment medium, Bitcoin is our preferred method of payment. This potentially makes it impossible for anyone to trace the purchase back to you. However, we will also accept cashier's checks. We hope to offer payment processing for major credit cards, but may require a third-party processor in order to keep the required level of anonymity for our customers.
All systems running Linux
All operational systems run hardened implementations of Linux, avoiding malware and vulnerability concerns with common closed-source systems.
Authorization logs deleted after 24 hours
This means that authorization logs are only usable for short-term troubleshooting, and cannot be subpoenaed because there is literally nothing to subpoena.
Access servers use read-only file systems
All of our access servers run a read-only file system. This prevents any type of logging, even by us!
Customer traffic mixed with Tor transit traffic
This makes it even harder for an external entity to track traffic leaving our servers, as we are operating as a Tor exit node and allowing non-customer traffic to also exit from our servers.
A portion of proceeds used to improve the Tor network and allow free VPN access
A portion of OneFourFive's profits will be donated to non-customer Tor traffic, increasing the overall speed of the Tor network. Additionally, we will devote a portion of our VPN connection licenses to free, unpaid VPN access to help people who may be unable to purchase access.
Authorization servers log to a RAM disk
This means that all logs are erased at system shutdown, and cannot be retrieved from disk using hardware forensics.
Incorporated in BVI
High level of secrecy, resistance to foreign confiscation, and flexibility in corporate structure make a BVI company hard to man-handle.

Who Are You?

FAQ

General

Why are you posting up your name, if you believe in anonymity so much?
In a nutshell, someone has to be the figurehead, and that person can't be anonymous.
Why OneFourFive?
This is a direct reference to the First, Fourth, and Fifth amendments to the Constitution of the United States of America. I feel strongly that all people, worldwide, are entitled to these rights, and I am seeing them be eroded day by day by hostile governments. This is my way of helping ensure that people always have a voice.
Why should I be concerned about anonymity if I have nothing to hide?
There is one good short answer to this, and a few excellent long ones. For the short answer, first and foremost, invasion of privacy is a reduction of rights, and reduction of rights is a 'ratcheting' process, meaning it only goes one way. Here, however, are a few longer retorts to this common position:

Are you going to run a Tor exit node?
Yes. We plan to donate a portion of proceeds to bandwidth for Tor, in the hopes that this will help in some small way to speed up Tor overall. This policy also serves to beef up the anonymity of the entire system by mixing in random Tor traffic with actual customer traffic.
What is the money going to be used for?
The majority will go towards development costs for the storefront, legal costs, and licensing fees. The rest will be used to pay operating costs for the first year of operation. If we exceed our goal, any additional funds will go preferentially towards speeding up development so that we can launch sooner.

Product Specific

How much will access cost?
The target is roughly $5 per month for unlimited, uncensored, unfettered access. Daily average prices will be higher for short terms (such as one day) and lower for long terms (such as one year).
Will there be free accounts?
Yes. We will always donate a portion of our licenses to free access.
What operating systems do you plan to support?
At launch, we plan to support SSL VPN (with a custom OpenVPN client) for Windows, Linux, and MacOS X. We will also support iOS devices with third-party OpenVPN clients, but will not provide the client due to iTunes licensing policies.
When do you expect the service to go live?
The target is Q1 of 2014. However, this may shift to sooner or later depending upon whether we meet or exceed our funding goals.
